Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Termite infestations can manifest in numerous ways, and recognizing the signs early is key to minimizing damage. Many homeowners in Grayslake might overlook subtle indicators, allowing termite colonies to grow and cause significant structural harm. Understanding these common problems can empower you to seek professional help sooner.

Subterranean Termite Activity and Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites are the most common type found in Illinois, including the Grayslake area. These termites live in underground colonies and build distinctive mud tubes to travel between their colony and their food source (your home's wood). These tubes, about the width of a pencil, are typically found on foundation walls, floor joists, and other structural elements. Finding these mud tubes almost always indicates the presence of active subterranean termites.

We address subterranean termite activity by first conducting a thorough inspection to locate all active mud tubes and identify the main areas of infestation. Our treatment methods for subterranean termites often involve applying a liquid termiticide barrier around the perimeter of your home's foundation. This barrier is designed to eliminate termites as they attempt to return to their colony or access your home. Additionally, we may use baiting systems strategically placed to attract termites, which then carry the toxic bait back to the colony, leading to its eventual collapse. Our comprehensive approach ensures that both the foraging termites and the colony are targeted.

Drywood Termite Infestations and Frass

While less common in Illinois than subterranean termites, drywood termites can still cause significant damage. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites live entirely within the wood they infest and do not require contact with the soil. A key sign of a drywood termite infestation is the presence of frass, which are tiny, pellet-like fecal droppings the termites leave behind as they tunnel through wood. This frass is often found in small piles near infested wood, such as door frames, window sills, or furniture.

Treating drywood termite infestations requires a different approach than treating subterranean termites. Since they live within the wood, treatments often involve localized applications directly into the infested wood. This might include drilling small holes and injecting dusts or liquids, or in some cases, using fumigation for widespread infestations. Our technicians are skilled in identifying drywood termite activity and determining the most effective localized or whole-structure treatment method to eradicate these pests and protect your wooden structures.

Evidence of Termite Swarmers

Termite swarmers, also known as alates, are the reproductive members of a termite colony. They are often mistaken for flying ants. Swarming typically occurs during certain times of the year when conditions are favorable, often after rain. The purpose of swarming is for these reproductive termites to leave the established colony, mate, and form new colonies. Finding discarded wings near windows, doors, or light fixtures is a strong indicator that a termite colony is present nearby and that a swarm has recently occurred or is underway.

The presence of termite swarmers or their discarded wings is a clear sign of an active termite colony in or around your property. While the swarmers themselves don't cause structural damage, their presence indicates that a mature, damaging colony is nearby. If you see swarmers, it's crucial to contact us immediately for a professional inspection. We will locate the source of the swarm and implement a targeted treatment plan to eliminate the parent colony and prevent new colonies from forming on your property. Addressing the active colony is the priority to stop ongoing damage.

Hollow-Sounding or Damaged Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving the exterior surface intact. This means that a piece of wood might look perfectly normal from the outside but be severely damaged within. Tapping on suspicion wood with a screwdriver can reveal if it sounds hollow or feels soft and spongy. This is a classic sign of significant termite damage beneath the surface.

If we discover hollow-sounding or damaged wood during our inspection, we will first confirm that the damage is indeed caused by termites. Then, depending on the extent of the damage and the location of the infestation, we will proceed with the appropriate treatment to eliminate the active termites. Following successful eradication, it's important to consider repairing or replacing the damaged wood to restore the structural integrity and appearance of your property. We can advise on the severity of the damage and necessary next steps for repairs.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

In severe cases of termite infestation, the structural integrity of your home can be compromised, leading to noticeable sagging in floors or ceilings. This occurs when termites have extensively damaged the support beams, joists, and other wooden structural elements. Sagging floors or ceilings are a serious indicator of advanced termite activity and require immediate professional attention.

If your property exhibits sagging floors or ceilings due to suspected termite damage, our initial focus is on confirming the presence of termites and determining the extent of structural compromise. We will then implement aggressive treatment strategies to eliminate the active termite infestation. Once the termites are eradicated, it is imperative to address the structural damage immediately. This will likely involve consulting with a qualified builder or contractor to repair or replace the compromised structural components. Addressing termite infestations before they reach this stage is crucial for preventing costly and extensive repairs.

Tight-Fitting Doors and Windows

As termites consume wood, they can cause it to swell and warp. This can result in doors and windows becoming difficult to open or close, fitting tightly within their frames. While this issue can be caused by other factors like humidity changes, if combined with other signs of termite activity, it can indicate an infestation.

If we notice tight-fitting doors and windows that appear related to termite activity, we will conduct a focused inspection of the surrounding wooden structures. If termites are confirmed as the cause, we will include these areas in our treatment plan to eliminate the termites responsible for the damage and prevent further warping. While addressing the termites will stop future damage, any existing swelling or warping may require adjustments or repairs to the doors and windows themselves.

Clicking Sounds within Walls

Although it might sound like something out of a horror movie, some homeowners report hearing faint clicking sounds coming from within their walls. This sound is believed to be made by certain types of termites as they feed on wood or communicate within the colony. While not a definitive sign on its own, if you hear unusual clicking sounds, it warrants investigation, especially if combined with other potential termite indicators.

If you suspect termite activity based on unusual sounds, we can conduct a thorough inspection using specialized equipment like acoustic detectors to help pinpoint the source of the sounds and confirm the presence of termites. If termites are detected, we will develop a targeted treatment plan to address the infestation in the affected walls and surrounding areas. Early detection through sound or other means can significantly reduce the extent of damage.
